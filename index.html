Women Lens was born as a data-driven project of digital storytelling that wants to investigate which were the most influential women photographers throughout history, how they were geographically distributed and in which parallel occupations have been engaged.

Assuming that women are under-represented in the field of photography, as well as in many other artistic fields, Women Lens is a virtual room entirely dedicated to women photographers and their personal and professional stories. Photography has certainly represented the opening of new horizons, contributing to the emancipation of women. Hence, the slogan "A camera of oneâ€™s own" wants to emphasize the role of the camera as the generator of new visions, entirely female, of the world. Just as Virginia Woolf needed a room of her own to write, women photographers with their personal cameras witness a scenario in constant change that sees them more involved and independent.

The research underlines the role of photography in enhancing women's emancipation. The investigated sub-topics are the timespan perspective and how women photographers were distributed among the decades; the geographical perspective and in which countries women photographers were more active; the professional perspective and how women photographers' parallel occupations changed during the time and around different countries.

The project is a free digital transposition of the 2021 publication "Donne Fotografe", Foto Note series, Contrasto Books, curated by Clara Bouveresse and Sarah Moon. The chosen photographers are those of the publication and the dataset was built by integrating the texts of the volumes with the information in RDF format retrieved on Wikidata.
